StorPunk

Introduction

Core Principles

StorPunk implements a complete decentralized ecosystem based on cypherpunk principles: privacy through cryptography, individual sovereignty, and resistance to centralized control. While built on a foundation of configurable data storage, the network aims to provide a comprehensive suite of tools for digital sovereignty.

Privacy by Design

Unlike traditional cloud storage where corporations control your data and can be compelled to reveal it, StorPunk provides true data sovereignty through cryptographic privacy. Users maintain full control over their data lifecycle without relying on corporate promises or permission-based systems. Privacy is achieved through cryptography, not through data expiration.

In today's changing political climate, where free speech faces increasing challenges, proactive data protection becomes essential. StorPunk enables users to maintain control of their information regardless of external pressures.

Sustainable Economics

Centralized data storage creates vulnerability to complete data loss from infrastructure failures. Permanent decentralized storage networks face economic challenges from the ongoing cost of maintaining historical data indefinitely. StorPunk addresses both issues: distributed architecture prevents single points of failure, while temporary storage ensures economic sustainability by limiting data maintenance to defined periods.

The temporary data approach eliminates network bloat and ensures users always pay market rates for storage. Clients set expiration dates that match their actual needs, and nodes price storage accordingly. This eliminates the risk of accumulating outdated information while ensuring costs remain transparent and market-based, preventing cascade collapse scenarios.

DAO Governance

The project uses a multi-phase token sale where $PUNK-DAO tokens are sold at 1:1 with USDT. These tokens provide governance rights and can be claimed for native $PUNK tokens with tier-based multipliers. After claiming, tokens become soulbound (non-transferable) while retaining governance rights.

Development follows conservative treasury management with community voting via Snapshot.org. The Community Development Fund ensures ongoing ecosystem development and governance long after initial phases conclude.

Complete Ecosystem Vision

While data storage provides the foundational layer, StorPunk aims to create a complete ecosystem for digital sovereignty:

- Financial Infrastructure: A decentralized exchange supporting fiat and cryptocurrency trading without intermediaries

- Trading Platform: A general marketplace for goods and services operating without central authorities

- Communication Tools: Censorship-resistant social media and information exchange capabilities

- Economic Foundation: A native token with transaction capacity designed to rival traditional systems

Tiered Sale Structure

$PUNK-DAO tokens are sold at a 1:1 ratio with USDT. The tier in which you purchase determines your $PUNK network token multiplier at launch:

Token Sale Tiers & Multipliers

  • Tier 3: 700,001-1,350,000 USDT → 1 USDT = 1 $PUNK-DAO, with 4x $PUNK multiplier at launch
  • Tier 4: 1,350,001-2,350,000+ USDT → 1 USDT = 1 $PUNK-DAO, with 2x $PUNK multiplier at launch

Holding Mechanism

The system includes multiplier incentives for token retention. At network launch:

- Original purchasers who never transferred their $PUNK-DAO tokens receive an additional 2x multiplier on their $PUNK tokens. This doubles the tier multiplier - for example, a Tier 1 buyer (16x) who never transfers their tokens would receive a 32x multiplier (16x × 2x).

- Secondary buyers only receive the base tier multiplier without the additional 2x

- Claiming happens on Ethereum—the contract verifies your holding history automatically

Soulbound Governance

After claiming your $PUNK tokens, your $PUNK-DAO tokens become soulbound (non-transferable):

1. You call the claim function on Ethereum, providing your StorPunk address

2. Your $PUNK-DAO tokens become permanently locked to your wallet

3. A bridge service detects your claim and mints $PUNK on StorPunk

4. You keep full voting rights forever—governance stays on Ethereum

Purpose of soulbound mechanism: This creates separation between governance rights and token speculation. Only unclaimed tokens trade on markets, maintaining governance among participants with demonstrated commitment to the network's principles and development.

Cypherpunk Foundations: StorPunk's Philosophical and Technical Principles

StorPunk implements decentralized infrastructure based on cypherpunk principles: privacy through cryptography, individual sovereignty, and resistance to centralized control. This document outlines the philosophical foundations and technical approach that guide StorPunk's development.

FOUNDATIONAL PRINCIPLES

1. Cypherpunk Philosophy

"Privacy is necessary for an open society in the electronic age." — The Cypherpunk Manifesto

StorPunk embodies core cypherpunk principles: strong cryptography, peer-to-peer architecture, voluntary association, and resistance to censorship. The project builds systems designed to protect individual sovereignty against both corporate surveillance and state control.

By implementing cryptographic privacy for data storage and communication, StorPunk aims to provide users with control over their digital existence. Privacy is achieved through cryptography, not through data expiration mechanisms.

This approach represents a commitment to decentralization—both technical architecture and philosophical opposition to concentrated control, whether corporate or governmental.

2. Resistance to Censorship

In an era of expanding digital control, censorship-resistant infrastructure serves essential functions.

As digital environments face increasing regulation and surveillance, the need for infrastructure that resists centralized control grows more apparent. StorPunk creates systems designed to remain operational across varying regulatory conditions.

The architecture resists content filtering, metadata collection, and centralized control points. This technical approach supports access to information and communication regardless of external pressures or platform policies.

Beyond privacy protection, the network supports epistemic freedom—the ability to think, communicate, and access information without surveillance or censorship constraints.

3. Privacy Through Architecture

In digital systems, privacy should be fundamental to the architecture, not an optional feature.

StorPunk implements privacy at the architectural level through cryptographic protections. In digital environments, control over data determines sovereignty over that data. When data becomes accessible to others without consent, it can be used against individual interests.

The network aims to provide tools for digital sovereignty, enabling users to maintain control over their data and transactions. This approach reduces the need to move data between networks and risk exposure through multiple points of vulnerability.

As Eric Hughes wrote in the Cypherpunk Manifesto, "For privacy to be widespread it must be part of a social contract. People must come and together deploy these systems for the common good." StorPunk represents this collective deployment approach.

TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION

4. User Sovereignty and Self-Custody

Digital sovereignty requires control over both data and economic assets.

StorPunk implements a self-custody model where users maintain control of their data, metadata, encryption keys, and tokens. No third party—including StorPunk developers—can access user information or assets without explicit permission.

The focus on usable implementation means cryptographic protections are designed to be accessible to regular users, not just technical experts. The architecture aims to make privacy and censorship resistance available through practical interfaces.

The cypherpunk approach emphasizes working code that enables individuals to exercise digital rights across various environmental conditions.

5. Engineering for Resilience

Sustainable systems require engineering that remains viable under real-world conditions.

StorPunk's architecture prioritizes long-term operation through several technical choices:

- Rust implementation for memory safety and performance

- Temporary storage model that addresses data accumulation and hardware requirements

- Minimal transaction fees that support network operations without significant user cost

- Peer-to-peer design that avoids single points of failure

The temporary storage approach provides economic and operational benefits. By automatically removing expired data, the network avoids the storage accumulation that challenges permanent storage systems.

This practical engineering focus aims to ensure that censorship-resistant infrastructure remains available and sustainable regardless of external conditions.

ECONOMIC MODEL

6. Private Economic Exchange

Freedom requires both private communication and economic exchange capabilities.

StorPunk integrates native exchange capabilities, enabling peer-to-peer value transfer designed to resist financial surveillance and third-party interference. This creates economic infrastructure where transactions can remain private and accessible across geographic and political boundaries.

The native token uses minimal transaction fees to deter network manipulation while maintaining low costs.

By enabling direct exchanges between individuals without intermediaries, StorPunk supports economic interactions based on voluntary agreement rather than permission requirements.

This approach implements principles of cryptographic consent and reputation-based interaction systems.

7. Voluntary Economic Model

Genuine markets depend on voluntary exchange without coercion.

StorPunk implements a direct market model where node operators receive compensation from storage fees, eliminating centrally-issued token rewards. This creates a voluntary network where participants interact through mutual agreement.

The economic design relies on direct service payments, establishing a model where token value corresponds to actual network usage. Users pay for the storage they need, for the duration they require it, while operators provide service at freely determined prices.

This extends cypherpunk principles into economics, creating interaction based on cryptographic proof and consent rather than intermediation.

3. Market-Based Pricing: Clients pay for exactly what they need, for exactly how long they need it

4. User Control: Individuals determine data retention periods rather than relying on permanent storage defaults

Claim Process (Available only after StorPunk network launch):

1. StorPunk network launches and a separate claim contract is deployed on Ethereum

2. User calls the claim function on this new claim contract, providing their StorPunk network address

3. The claim contract verifies your $PUNK-DAO token balance and transaction history

4. Your $PUNK-DAO tokens immediately become soulbound (permanently non-transferable) via cross-contract integration

5. A bridge service detects the claim event and mints your $PUNK tokens on StorPunk at the appropriate multiplier

How Soulbound Works: The claim contract marks your address as having claimed, and the DAO contract's transfer function checks this status before allowing any transfers. This creates an on-chain enforcement mechanism that permanently prevents transfers while preserving governance rights.

Warning: Once you claim, your $PUNK-DAO tokens become permanently non-transferable, though they retain full governance rights. Only claim when you're ready to use the StorPunk network.

StorPunk DAO Constitution

Preamble

We, the members of the StorPunk Decentralized Autonomous Organization, unite under the principles of the Cypherpunk Manifesto to build and govern a system that empowers individual privacy, freedom, and sovereignty in the digital age.

Privacy is necessary for an open society. We build StorPunk to provide temporary, encrypted storage that leaves no permanent trace—a tool for those who value their right to selectively reveal information. Through cryptography and decentralized governance, we defend privacy not through words but through code.

This Constitution defines the governance framework while preserving the immutable core mission of StorPunk: creating a temporary storage network that implements cypherpunk principles of privacy, cryptographic protection, and individual sovereignty.

Key Constitutional Principles

- Privacy First: Default to anonymous, encrypted operations

- Individual Sovereignty: Empower users to control their data

- Decentralization: Resist centralized control or censorship

- Transparency: Open source code and verifiable operations

- Immutable Mission: Temporary storage with automatic expiration

Governance Structure

The StorPunk DAO operates through:

- Token-Based Voting: $PUNK-DAO holders vote on all governance proposals

- Snapshot Governance: Gasless voting through snapshot.org

- Soulbound Rights: Permanent voting rights even after claiming native tokens

- Community Fund: 40% of unsold tokens allocated for ongoing development

- Decentralized Decision Making: No central authority controls the network

Constitutional Protections

- Immutable Core Mission: The temporary storage focus cannot be changed

- Privacy Guarantees: User data remains encrypted and anonymous

- Censorship Resistance: No entity can block access to the network

- Open Source: All code must remain publicly verifiable

- Community Control: Token holders retain ultimate authority
